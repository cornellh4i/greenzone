Every spring, herders across Mongolia ask the same question: will there be enough grass this year? GreenZone was built to help answer it earlier and with more confidence, by turning decades of satellite imagery into a picture of rangeland health that updates year after year.

Our models look at vegetation indices like **NDVI** alongside precipitation, temperature, snow cover, and soil characteristics. Where the signal points to vegetation falling short of what the land can normally support, we flag it — long before the shortfall becomes visible on the ground.

This is the first in a series where we'll share:

- what the data is telling us about this season,
- how the models turn raw satellite pixels into rangeland health, and
- what it means for carrying capacity and degradation risk across the country's soums.
